Phishing is a type of cyber attack that involves tricking people 
into giving out sensitive information, 
such as passwords and credit card numbers,
 by pretending to be a trustworthy source. 
This is typically done through fake emails, 
social media messages, or websites that look legitimate, but are actually designed to steal personal information.

Here's how a phishing attack might work:

The attacker sends a fake email or message to a large group of people, pretending to be from a legitimate company or organization.

The message may contain a link to a fake website that looks exactly like the real thing, but is actually controlled by the attacker.

If the recipient clicks on the link and enters their login details or other sensitive information, 
the attacker can then use that information to gain access to the person's accounts or steal their identity.

To protect yourself from phishing attacks, it's important to be cautious when clicking 
on links in emails or messages, and to always verify the authenticity of websites before entering sensitive information.
 It's also a good idea to use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.



Phishing is a type of cyber attack that aims to steal sensitive information from individuals or organizations.
 It typically involves the attacker sending fake emails, social media messages, or text messages that appear to be
 from a legitimate source, but are actually designed to trick the recipient into giving out their login credentials, 
credit card numbers, or other sensitive information. The attackers may also create fake websites that look exactly like
 the real thing, in order to trick people into entering their information.


Phishing attacks can be difficult to detect, 
as the attackers often go to great lengths to make their
 messages and websites look legitimate. However, there are 
some signs to look out for, such as unusual sender addresses,
 unexpected attachments or links, and requests for personal information.
 To protect against phishing attacks, it's important to be cautious when
 clicking on links or entering information online, and to always verify the
 authenticity of websites before entering sensitive information. It's also a good idea to use strong,
 unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.
